Pi LED Control

A light-emitting diode (LED) typically has a forward voltage of between 1.8 and 3.3 volts. The simplest way to drive an LED is through a series resistor where energy is dissipated in the resistor as heat.

A LED has a voltage drop specified at the intended operating current. Ohm’s law and Kirchhoff’s circuit laws are used to calculate the appropriate resistor value, by subtracting the LED voltage drop from the supply voltage and dividing by the desired operating current.
